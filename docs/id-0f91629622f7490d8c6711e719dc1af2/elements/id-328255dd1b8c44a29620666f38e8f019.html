Open Data Management ()
Definition: Open Data Management refers to the practice of making data freely available, accessible, and usable by the public, without restrictive copyright or other controls, to encourage transparency, innovation, and collaboration. Source: Open Knowledge Foundation Source reference: https://okfn.org/opendata/ Example: Data.gov The U.S. government's open data portal that provides public access to high value, machine-readable datasets generated by the Executive Branch of the Federal Government. European Union Open Data Portal Provides access to data from the institutions and other bodies of the European Union. The data covers various topics such as the economy, employment, science, environment, and education. CKAN An open-source data portal platform used by governments, organizations, and communities worldwide to publish and share data. It provides tools to streamline publishing, sharing, finding, and using data.
